The INT/WFC Photometric Hα Survey (IPHAS)

Learn more » IPHAS is a 1800 deg2 survey of the Northern Galactic Plane at |b| < 5° in H‑alpha, r' and i',
using the Wide Field Camera (WFC) at the 2.5-metre Isaac Newton Telescope (INT).

Image: IC1396 in IPHAS (N. Wright)

New Galactic Supernova Remnants discovered with IPHAS

Read the paper on ADS » Five new supernova remnants have been discovered in our Galaxy using IPHAS H-alpha images and follow-up spectroscopy. The results are published in Sabin et al. 2013 (MNRAS).

Survey

IPHAS covers our Galaxy at -5° < b < +5° and 29° < l < 215°, down to a depth of r'≈21, i'≈20, Hα≈20 (Vega system).

Science

The IPHAS (r'-Hα, r'-i') colour-colour plane allows a range of rare and short-lived object types to be identified.
